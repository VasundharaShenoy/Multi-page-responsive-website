Lady Bird


“It’s Not Important To Be Right. It’s Only Important To Be True.”

Lady bird unlike other teen romance movies focuses more on the relationship between a mom and daughter. The movie is about a girl Christine McPherson who like most teens is rebellious and wants to do things her own way. She gives herself the name “lady bird” asking why she should stick to the name she was given by birth. She wants to escape her hometown Sacramento and go to a place where she can be more of herself, her mom is against her idea of moving away. They are both stubborn about their decisions and have a strong personality which leads to a lot of conflicts. They both are alike but at the same time are very different. The movie conveys powerful message on individuality. “I want you to be the very best version of yourself that you can be,” Lady Bird's mother says, to which Lady Bird responds, “What if this is the best version?”. We watch her battle with her mother, test out different friends and boyfriends and try to figure out who she is. In the whole movie it looked like she hated her hometown and all she wanted was to leave only in the end she realises how much she loved Sacramento.


The main message of the whole movie is to value our surroundings and enjoy the present that life gives us.
